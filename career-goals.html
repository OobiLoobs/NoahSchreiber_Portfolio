About Me

My career path has been anything but linear, and I think that's what makes it interesting. I came into the workforce with a creative foundation, a background in audio production, and a genuine love for storytelling through sound. Over time though, my experiences led me to a place I never expected: software engineering. Getting here required some honest self-reflection, a willingness to let go of plans I had held onto for years, and the commitment to starting fresh when something wasn't right.

Where I've Been

I graduated with an Associate's degree in Recording Arts and Technology, focusing much of my effort on audio post-production for film and television. During that time, I had the opportunity to mix the score for a student film and build relationships with working professionals at respected studios in the industry. Those conversations were some of the most valuable for my career, not because they confirmed my path, but because they gave me an honest picture of what that life actually looked like. The work was creative and meaningful, but even accomplished professionals at the top of the field faced unpredictable income and no guarantee of continuous work. I came to understand that the lifestyle the industry demanded wasn't compatible with the life I wanted to build.

Where I Am Now

After my employer was acquired by a larger university, our A/V and IT departments were combined, leading me to transition into an IT support role. Right around that same time, I started building a video game in my free time. Having that project to come home to made a real difference for my mental health, and sticking with the unexpected change that I hadn't planned for. As I settled into IT work, I found myself genuinely enjoying it. The logical thinking, troubleshooting, and satisfaction of solving a problem efficiently. A few months in, a colleague asked me to help with database requests, which pushed me to start learning SQL. Meanwhile, learning to code for my game had lit something in me. I kept setting aside the creative work just to write more code, and that feeling told me something important.

Where I'm Going

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering. My short-term focus is on strengthening my foundation in software development fundamentals, while expanding my experience across multiple languages and development environments. Long term, I'm keeping my options open. I'm genuinely interested in the breadth of what software engineering has to offer, and I wouldn't rule out eventually finding a way to bring my creative background into it. Whether that means audio programming or tools development in the game industry, or possibly diving into the tech field with software and app development. I'm building skills I enjoy using, staying open to where they lead, and trusting that the rest will follow.
